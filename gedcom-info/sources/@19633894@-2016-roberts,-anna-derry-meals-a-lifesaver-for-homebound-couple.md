## Text

> PALM BEACH GARDENS
>
> <br/>
>
> Meals a lifesaver for home bound couple
>
> <br/>
>
> Derry Roberts must stay home to care for his wife, who has Alzheimer's.
>
> <br/>
>
> By Sara Peters
>
> Palm Beach Post Staff Writer
>
> <br/>
>
> There were times Derry and Anna Robers had no food in the house, but Derry couldn't leave his wife home alone while he ran to the grocery store.
>
> <br/>
>
> It is hard for Derry, 80, to get Anna, 77, in the car now that she's in the late stages Alzheimer's disease Enter Meals on Wheels of the Palm Beaches.
>
> <br/>
>
> The nonprofit organisation's volunteers started delivering meals to the couple's home five days a week every week about two months ago. They've even providers dog food and grooming for the couple's playful, 3-year-old Shih Tzu, Oakley.
>
> <br/>
>
> Anna's meals are paid for by a different nonprofit, and Derry receives his for less than $30 a month.
>
> <br/>
>
> "They've been a godsend. They've been great," Derry Roberts said as he sat in the living room of their Palm Beach Gardens home Monday. "They're keeping us alive."
>
