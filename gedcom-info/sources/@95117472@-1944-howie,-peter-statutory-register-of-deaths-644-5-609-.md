## Text

> 1944, DEATHS in the DISTRICT of SPRINGBURN in the BURGH of GLASGOW
>
> <br/>
>
> No. 609: 
>
> Name and Surname: Peter Howie
>
> Profession: Provisions Merchant's Clerk
>
> Condition: Widower of: 1st Annie Boyle; 2nd Helen Niddrie or Wills; 3rd Agnes Christie or Wilson
>
> When and Where Died: 1944 April Twentieth 5h 10m A.M. 133 Balornock Road, Glasgow. 
>
> Usual Residence: 861 Cathcart Road, Glasgow
>
> Sex: M
>
> Age: 83 years
>
> <br/>
>
> Name, and Maiden Surname of Mother: Margaret Howie (afterwards married to Alexander Taylor, Stevedore) (deceased)
>
> <br/>
>
> Cause of Death: Cerebral Haemorrhage
>
> As certified by: L. Chalmers-Smith, M.B., Ch.B.
>
> <br/>
>
> Name and Qualification of Informant: Peter Howie, Son; 18 Halbert Street, Glasgow.
>
> <br/>
>
> When and Where Registered: 1944 April 20th at Glasgow
>
> Registrar: J. Gilligan (assistant registrar)
>

## Notes

> 133 Balornock Road, Glasgow is Stobhill Hospital, which was opened in 1904 as a Poor Law hospital for the City of Glasgow. By 1944 it had developed into a substantial general hospital serving the north of Glasgow.
